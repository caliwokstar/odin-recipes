Cheeseburger & Chips

Description

Homemade burgers and chips beat shop-bought every time. Why not make double and freeze a batch?

Freezable (Can be frozen uncooked)

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¼kg lean beef mince
  • 1 onion
  • 140g breadcrumb
  • 100g mature cheddar
  • small bunch parsley
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp mild chilli powder
  • 2 ½kg bag of potato
  • 3 tbsp plain flour
  • 10 tsp olive or sunflower oil
  • burger buns

Method:

Burgers

  1. Add the burger mixture in a large bowl, combine:
    • Beef mince
    • Finely chopped/grated onion
    • Breadcrumbs
    • Grated cheddar
    • Chopped parsley
    • Worcestershire sauce
    • Egg
    • Chilli powder
    • Salt and pepper
  2. Mix well
    • Use your hands or a spoon to mix until everything is evenly combined.
  3. Shape into patties
    • Divide the mixture into 8 portions. Shape each one into a patty about 2 cm thick. Place them on a tray.
  4. Chill (optional but helpful)
    • Refrigerate the patties for 20–30 minutes to help them hold their shape while cooking.
  5. Cook the burgers
    • Heat 2 tsp oil in a pan or on a grill.
    • Cook each burger for about 5–6 minutes per side on medium heat, or until cooked through.
    • Add cheese slices (optional) and let melt in the last minute of cooking.
    • Toast the burger buns lightly if desired.

Chips

  1. Prep the chips
    • Wash and cut the potatoes into even-sized chips.
    • Pat them dry with a clean towel.
  2. Coat with flour
    • In a large bowl, toss the chips with 3 tbsp plain flour. This helps make them crispier.
  3. Preheat the oven
    • Set oven to 200°C (fan) or 220°C (conventional).
    • Line two baking trays with parchment or lightly oiled foil.
  4. Bake the chips
    • Spread chips out on trays. Drizzle with the 8 tsp oil and toss to coat.
    • Bake for 35–40 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and crisp.
    • Season with salt after baking.
